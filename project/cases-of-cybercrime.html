The PlayStation Network Attack


Sony is one of the biggest companies in the world, but even they have fallen vicitms to Cybercrime attacks. Back in 2011, the PSN hack took place, resulting in unauthorised access into the PlayStation network.


This attack drew outrage as approximately 77 million accounts were compromised. A huge amount of consoles and accounts were also prevented from accessing the network. In May 2011, Sony admiited the persoanlly identifiable information from the accounts had been exposed. What caused more outrage was this event lasted 23 days, it was one of the largest cyber attacks of all time. The event led to around $1781 million in costs for Sony and caused multiple lawsuits.

The 2014 Yahoo Attack


In 2014, Yahoo became the victim of one of the biggest data breaches in history. Approximately 500 million accounts were hacked by a state-sponsored actor. Criminals were said to have stolen everything from names and email addresses to telephone numbers, passwords, and date of birth details, the theft was the biggest known cyber breach recorded at the time.


Although the attack officially took place in 2004, Yahoo only discovered the incursion after later reports were filed relating to a secondary breach. All the reports issued turned out to be false, but the investigation revealed significant details about the 2014 attack

The NASA Cyber Attack


This was a Cyber Attack which took place during 1999.The crucial computers of NASA were breached and were shutdown for roughly 21 days.


During this attack around 1.7 million pieces of software were downloaded onto the computers. This resulted in the space company paying around $41,000 in repairs. What made this attack so bizarre was the criminal who was guilty for the attack. A 15 year old Computer hacker was found guilty and was senteneced 6 months in jail. During the sentence the young boy was informed to write letters of apology to NASA administrators and secretary of defense.
